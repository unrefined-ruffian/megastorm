# Anti-Incumbent

**What this lens does:** Maps every attribute the incumbents in a category share, then systematically inverts them to find the breakout product hiding in the negative space. Incumbents converge over time — same materials, same aesthetic, same messaging, same vibe. That convergence IS the opportunity. Every attribute they all agree on is an attribute nobody is questioning.

**The Liquid Death principle:** Bottled water incumbents converged on plastic, clear, small, round, and "life/vitality" messaging. Liquid Death inverted every single attribute: aluminum, opaque, tall, aggressive, death/skulls. The result wasn't a better water — it was a *different category* wearing water's clothes.

## When NOT to Use

- There is no established category or incumbent to invert — this needs a mature market with convergent players
- The problem is about improving an existing product within its current positioning, not repositioning against incumbents
- The category is already fragmented with no dominant playbook to invert — anti-incumbent needs a playbook to be anti-

## How to Run It

1. **Map the category playbook.** List 6-10 attributes that ALL major incumbents share. Go beyond the obvious (material, price) into the emotional and cultural (vibe, who it's for, where it's sold, what it signals).

2. **For each attribute, ask: convention or load-bearing?**
   - **Convention:** "This is how it's done" — arbitrary, historical, never questioned. These are the safest inversions and often the most powerful.
   - **Load-bearing:** This attribute exists because it serves a real functional or psychological need. Inverting this is either genius or suicide — the question is whether the need can be served a different way.

3. **Invert each attribute.** Don't just pick the opposite — pick the most *interesting* opposite. "Not plastic" could be glass, aluminum, paper, biodegradable. Which inversion creates the most tension with the category?

4. **Read the inversions as a product.** Do the inverted attributes form a coherent identity? The magic of Liquid Death is that ALL the inversions pointed the same direction — toward a single, unmistakable brand. If the inversions are incoherent (some point luxury, some point punk), you need to pick a lane.

5. **Find the audience that's been waiting.** Every category playbook implicitly excludes someone. The anti-incumbent product is for the people the incumbents weren't designing for — the ones who looked at the whole shelf and felt nothing.

## Agent Prompt

> "Apply the Anti-Incumbent framework to this problem brief.
>
> Step 1: Map the category playbook — list 6-10 attributes that ALL major incumbents in this space share. Include functional attributes (materials, format, price), experiential attributes (where it's encountered, how it's used), and cultural attributes (vibe, who it's for, what it signals about the buyer).
>
> Step 2: For each attribute, classify it as CONVENTION (arbitrary, historical, never questioned) or LOAD-BEARING (serves a real functional or psychological need). This distinction determines which inversions are genius and which are suicide.
>
> Step 3: Invert each attribute — pick the most interesting opposite, not just the logical one. Present this as a table: Attribute | Incumbents | Anti-Incumbent | Convention or Load-Bearing?
>
> Step 4: Read the inversions as a product. Do they form a coherent identity? If so, name the vibe. If not, identify which inversions should be kept and which create incoherence.
>
> Step 5: Who is the audience that's been waiting? Every incumbent playbook implicitly excludes someone. Name them.
>
> Write your Reframe (what the incumbent convergence reveals about the category's blind spot), Ideas (the 2-3 strongest inversions and how they combine into a product or feature), and Wild Card (the load-bearing attribute that could be inverted if you served the underlying need a different way — the scariest and most interesting move)."

```markdown
- [The strongest inversion cluster and what it becomes as a product/feature]

```

## What Good Output Looks Like

- The attribute list goes beyond functional into emotional and cultural — "vibe" and "what it signals" are as important as "material" and "price"
- The convention vs. load-bearing distinction is sharp — it should be clear WHY each attribute exists
- The inversions are specific and interesting, not just "the opposite" — aluminum can, not just "not plastic"
- The coherence check reveals whether the inversions point in one direction or scatter
- The excluded audience is named specifically enough to feel like real people, not a demographic
- The wild card is genuinely scary — the load-bearing inversion that everyone would say is impossible
